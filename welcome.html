Tonight's Featured Record
Fleetwood Mac — Rumours (1977)
Their eleventh studio album and easily their best known, Rumours was recorded while the band was falling apart in real time. Two couples within the band breaking up simultaneously and everyone at each other's throats...like, constantly. The result? One of the most iconic pop records of all time that's sold over 40 million copies and won a Grammy for Album of the Year. See, Chaos can be productive.
There are multiple elements to this record that make it ideal for vinyl listening. The warmth and texture of the guitars, whether electric or acoustic, are noticeable across multiple tracks. Vinyl also helps to highlight the unique characteristics of each singer during the three-part harmonies between Stevie Nicks, Lindsey Buckingham, and Christine McVie (RIP). Regardless of the medium, the pop-sensibilities of this classic album rub up against some deeply personal lyrics. We really enjoy this album and we're grateful to be able to listen to it with you. And even if you didn't vote for it, who cares the world's going to end soon anyways. Cheers!
Tonight's Cocktails
Side A
McNicks Spritz
Named for Stevie Nicks, who apparently liked to drink both tequila and wine simultaneously. It's no Jamo and Guiness but hey, props to her. It's a citrusy tequila-and-white-wine spritz that combines both.
Side B
Black Velvet
Inspired by Stevie's iconic West Village look — layered fabrics, fringe, and crushed velvet. Two ingredients, two very different worlds: Guinness meets dry sparkling wine. Dark, elegant, and unexpectedly bubbly. It's way better than it sounds.
